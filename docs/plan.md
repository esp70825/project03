## Risk Analysis with TAME

| Risk | Impact | Probability | Score |
| -------- | -------- | -------- | -------- | 
| Concern with layout distortion   | 3   | 3 | 9 |
| Visual clutter and organization   | 3   | 2 | 6 |
| Fatigue | 4 | 4 | 16 |
| Procrastination | 4 | 3 | 12 |

### Risk 1: Layout Distortion
During the previous personal webpage project, I noticed that the site did not render as I expected when accessed on a mobile device. I am concerned that the layout for this paint crossover webstite may not render cleanly, potentially resulting in an unreadable user interface.
*   **TAME Strategy:** **Mitigate**

I will first develop the website's main structure using HTML, then visual styling using CSS. Once the application renders successfully on desktop, I will evaluate the mobile layout and implement necessary changes. I have identified W3Schools as a primary research reference in case I run into any issues. To mitigate potential layout risks, I am proactively researching beforehand to understand what the best practices are.

### Risk 2: Page Organization
Displaying cross-references for multiple competitive paint brands (Sherwin-Williams, Behr, PPG, Glidden, and Kilz) against their Lowe's alternatives introduces an extensive volume of textual and visual information. This data density creates a distinct risk of producing a cluttered, overwhelming user interface.
*   **TAME Strategy:** **Mitigate**
 
 I have already drawn out a physical layout design to show how the crossover data will be organized. To ensure optimal user experience, I will implement adequate spacing between products, high contrast colors, and distinct headings. I will mitigate the risk of user interface confusion by strictly aligning the digital crossover with my predesigned layout.   

### Risk 3: Fatigue
Although I have dedicated blocks of open time each afternoon after my 2:00 PM work shift, my early 5:30 AM start time introduces significant physical and mental fatigue. This creates a persona risk where my want to rest could lead to procrastination, shortened development timeline, and thus impacting the quality of the final project.  
*   **TAME Strategy:** **Eliminate**

To eliminate this risk, I will implement a structured evening schedule with a dedicated rest window. I will immideatly begin my project tasks following dinner (my family has an early dinner at around 4 PM). I will utilize my evening routine as an incentive, postponing my routine comforts like showering until I have completed my daily project goals. I am also driven by the motivation to finish the semester strong, I will effectively eliminate development fatigue by maintaining this schedule.
